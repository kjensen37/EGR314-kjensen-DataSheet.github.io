## Microcontroller/Module Startup Tip
Based on my experience getting this board working, these are the things that I would look into. **Power Indicating LED:** One of the greatest and most usable features that you can have on your board. It is the indicator for whether or not your board is receiving power, and if it is, whilst the LED is not on, then you know you have a short somewhere. **Test Points:** This is the one feature that I overlooked, which would have made testing my voltages and continuities a lot easier. **Utilize Silkscreen:** I cannot emphasize this one enough, whether it be labeling buttons or LEDS, having a dedicated LED with power next to it, or having a button with BOOT above it, not only helps you, but it would help teammates, professors, and TAs locate vital components or LED indicators. **Extra Parts:** You more than likely will fry something. I have fried more than my fair share of components. Don't be afraid to use that budget; you were given a large budget for a reason. Do not be afraid to buy extra parts. **Check Component Sizes:** I fell victim to this myself, my temperature sensor required very small pads that JLC could not print. Causing me to have to redesign and order a new temperature sensor. Do not make the same mistake, I wouldnt go smaller than 07
